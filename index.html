Dr. Norman Borlaug

He was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production termed the Green Revolution.

Borlaug was awarded multiple honors for his work, including the Nobel Peace Prize, the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal.

1914 Norman Borlaug was born.

1937 Norman Borlaug got married.

1941 Norman got his Ph.D. in plant pathology.

1961 Norman created semi-dwarf wheat that he gave to Mexico to try out.

1965 Borlaug’s dwarf wheat was beginning shipped to Pakistan and India.

1968 Norman Borlaug made seeds and fertilizer available to India Because of Norman’s accomplishment in Pakistan, they have become independent in growing grain.

1970 Norman was given the Nobel Peace Prize for starting the Green Revolution.

1972 Borlaug traveled to China with Richard Nixon to spread the Green Revolution.

1977 He won the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

1984 He was given the position of the Distinguished Professor of International Agriculture at Texas A & M University.

1986 Borlaug created the World Food Prize. Norman Borlaug was called to Africa so he could bring the Green Revolution to Africa.

2002 Norman won the Public Welfare Medal from the U.S. National Academy of Sciences.

2004 He was awarded the National Medal of Science.

2006 He was awarded the National Gold Medal.

2007 Borlaug won the APS Award of Distinction. Norman's wife,Margaret, died. Norman was presented with the Congressional Medal of Honor

2009 Norman Borlaug died.
